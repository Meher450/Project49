Intersting Facts About Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camel can move easily across the sand because of its specially designed feet.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

Intersting Facts About Chameleon

  1. Chameleons actually change their colour due to a number of different factors, including their mood, changes in light or temperature, or the humidity of their environment.
  2. Chameleons’ eyesight has developed both to help them catch prey and, as we’ve seen, to help them escape predators. They can focus their eyes relatively well, meaning they can judge distances and spot prey between 5 and 10 metres.
  3. They can move their eyes separately, with each eye having a field of 180 degrees. This means they can see 360 degrees if they need to!
  4. The largest chameleon by length is the Malagasy giant chameleon, also known as Oustalets’s Chameleon. Some members of this species have been measured at almost 70cm, and are one of the species endemic to Madagascar (meaning they don’t naturally live anywhere else).

Intersting Facts About Pigeon

  • Pigeons have fought alongside humans in World War I and II.
  • Pigeons can inbreed exactly up to 21 times before the effects become detrimental.
  • Pigeons always lay at least 2 eggs with each one being a male and a female.
  • Pigeons are an invasive species, shrinking the population of smaller birds when theirs increases.

Intersting Facts About Teddy-Bear

  1. The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
  2. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  3. The Teddy Bears' Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step.
  4. There is a dedicated magazine to teddy bears. This magazine is called ‘Teddy Bear and Friends’. It has about 40,000 subscribers… .

Intersting Facts About Panda

  • Panda have great camouflage for their environment.
  • Mother pandas keep contact with their cub nearly 100% of the time during their first month - with the cub resting on her front and remaining covered by her paw, arm or head.
  • Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.
  • Although pandas are 99% vegetarian their digestive system is typical of a carnivore. For the 1% of their diet that isn't bamboo, pandas eat eggs, small animals, carrion, and forage in farmland for pumpkin, kidney beans, wheat and domestic pig food.

Intersting Facts About Flying-Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus. (Females make a much quieter sound by flicking their wings.) Cicadas don’t sing at night, so don’t blame them if you can’t sleep.
  3. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes. Keep on the lookout.
  4. Females may be attracted to the sound of motors. So you get to watch your dad, mom or older sibling get swarmed while they’re using the lawn mower or power tools.
